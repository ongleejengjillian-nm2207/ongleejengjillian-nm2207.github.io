Tourism

How has Covid-19 affected tourism in Singapore?


As seen in the map above, Singapore saw a significant fall in international visitor arrivals in 2021 compared to pre-pandemic levels. Many of these markets belong in Asia and Oceania. The most largest change in percentage was from Australia with a 99% decrease in tourist arrivals. This is considerable change as around 1.14 million Australians visited Singapore in 2019. With almost no tourists coming in from Australia, it is expected that Singapore's tourism will take a hit.

To find out more, lets take a look at the line chart below!

This chart clearly shows the changes in the total Toursim receipts in Singapore. As we can there was a huge dip in tourism receipts from 2019 to 2021. Compared to the whopping $26.94 billion generated in 2018, only $1.89 billion was generated in 2019.

But hold up! That still seems quite a lot is it not? But no, Singapore's is largely dependent on tourism as it accounts for roughly 5.1% of the Gross Domestic Product(GDP)!

Which industries were hit hard?

Now it is important to note that many industries were greatly affected as no one anticipated the long term severity of the pandemic.

These 2 bar graphs represent the months September in the years 2019 and 2021. With such a drastic drop of $300 million from 2019 to 2021, it can be inferred that many individuals working in such related industries would have been laid off. Hence we can see that Covid-19 not only had an adverse impact on industries, but it also affected the lives of working individuals.

Why should we care?

Why should we ordinary citizens care about such facts? Well it is impossible not to since Covid-19 has so deeply affected our daily lives and lifestyles. With a limitations placed on our social mobility, we too are unable to partake in touristy activities abroad.

This pie chart depict the frequency of visit for the year 2019. It is evident that many tourists frequent Singapore on a repeat basis, which implies that Singapore has given tourists a good impression, so much so that 51% of said tourists have chosen to Singapore again!

Furthermore this next pie chart gives us a breakdown on the main purposes of visit. A large majority of which either visited Sinagpore for Holiday or to visit their friends and family.

Hence this bring us back to the main question: Why should we care? Well from the statistics alone we can infer that the majority of tourists that come to Singapore on a repeat basis are likely from our neighbouring country, Malaysia. Despite this close proximity, those with family in Malaysia and Singapore were separated from Covid-19 restrictions. Therefore looking beyond the economic impact the Covid-19 brought on tourism, Covid-19 had more importantly removed one's freedom of mobility and the ability to connect with loved ones.
